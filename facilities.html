H.O.D. Clubs & Societies

A club is an association of people united by a common interest or goal bringing together like-minded people for individual or collective growth and development in a particular area. A society, on the other hand, is a group of persons associated together by a common interest, united by a common vow, holding the same belief or opinion.

In conformity with the Ministry of Education's policy to award schools in non-academic areas, St. Francis Girls' Secondary School, Suwerwa takes center stage in achieving this. Every Wednesday from 4:00 pm to 5:00 pm is set aside for club activities and meetings held under the guidance of dedicated patrons.

Christian Union

Our one society the spiritual heartbeat of the school. The Christian Union nurtures faith, fellowship, and discipleship among students and staff alike.

Why Clubs Matter

The main aims of clubs and societies at SFGS include educating, stimulating thinking, doing research, entertaining, developing creativity, rendering services to oneself and society, providing avenues for socialization, helping students grow spiritually and morally, and helping them develop and exploit their gifts and talents.

All students are encouraged to join at least two clubs: one academic and one non-academic. Our school has continuously excelled in club-related activities and competitions both nationally and internationally participating in Wildlife, President's Award Scheme, Mathematics, World Scholars, Chess, Red Cross, St. John Ambulance, and Science clubs among many others.
