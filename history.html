Our History

Over five decades of academic excellence, Catholic values, and transformative education — from a humble beginning in 1972 to one of Kenya's leading girls' national schools.

Established 1972    "ORA ET LABORA — PRAY AND WORK!"

Girls' School Status

St. Francis Girls' Secondary School, Suwerwa is a Girls' National Boarding School located in Kitale, Trans-Nzoia County, Kenya, with a rich and inspiring history. Founded in 1972 by the Franciscan Sisters of St. Elizabeth under the patronage of St. Francis of Assisi, the school was established with a powerful mission: to provide quality education for girls in the Franciscan tradition, nurturing them into women of faith, integrity, and service. What began as a small institution has grown into one of Kenya's most celebrated girls' schools.

A Journey Through Time

1972

The Founding of SFGS

St. Francis Girls' Secondary School, Suwerwa was established by the Franciscan Sisters of St. Elizabeth. The school opened with Form 1 students and a dedicated teaching staff under the patronage of St. Francis of Assisi — with a powerful vision to provide quality, holistic, Catholic education to girls across the region and prepare them for higher education and life.

Foundation
1970s

Early Growth & Christian Formation

In its early years, the school built a steady reputation for discipline and Christian formation. Student enrolment grew steadily as families across Trans-Nzoia and Western Kenya heard of the school's commitment to holistic, faith-centred education for girls. The Franciscan Sisters instilled a culture of prayer, hard work, and service that would define the school for generations.

Growth
1980s

First KCSE Cohort & Expansion

A landmark decade: the first KCSE cohort sat their examinations, establishing the school's academic tradition. Classrooms, dormitories, and the school chapel were expanded to accommodate growing numbers. The school began building its strong academic reputation alongside its commitment to spiritual and character formation.

Expansion
1990s

National School Status

A pivotal decade: St. Francis Girls' Secondary School was elevated to National School status, attracting students from across Kenya. New laboratories, a library, and sports facilities were constructed to match national standards. The school's reputation for producing well-rounded, disciplined young women attracted students from every corner of the country.

National Status
2000s

National & International Excellence

Continued excellence in KCSE. The school began sending students to national and international competitions in academics, sports, music, and drama. SFGS cemented its place as one of Kenya's finest girls' schools, producing graduates who went on to excel in medicine, education, public service, journalism, and the Church.

Excellence
Today

A Living Legacy

St. Francis Girls Suwerwa has over 1,200 students and is recognized as one of Kenya's leading girls' national schools. Alumni of SFGS serve with distinction across Kenya and beyond in medicine, law, education, public service, and religious life.

Alumni Legacy

SFGS Today

St. Francis Girls' Secondary School, Suwerwa today stands as a thriving institution with over 1,200 students, offering a wide range of academic programmes and extracurricular activities including sports, music, drama, and clubs. Despite significant growth over the decades, the school's core commitment — to provide holistic, Catholic-based education that produces women of faith, excellence, and service — has never wavered.
