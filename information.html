Our History

Over four decades of academic excellence, Christian values, and transformative education from a humble beginning in 1972 to one of Kenya's leading Catholic girls' national schools.

St. Francis Girls' Secondary School, Suwerwa is a Girls' National Boarding School located in Kitale, Trans-Nzoia County, Kenya, with a rich and inspiring history. Founded in 1972 by the Franciscan Sisters of St. Elizabeth under the patronage of St. Francis of Assisi, the school was established with a powerful mission: to provide quality, holistic Catholic education for girls. What began as a small institution has grown into one of Kenya's most celebrated girls' national schools.

A Journey Through Time

1972

The Founding of SFGS

St. Francis Girls' Secondary School, Suwerwa was established by the Franciscan Sisters of St. Elizabeth. The school opened with Form 1 students and a dedicated teaching staff under the patronage of St. Francis of Assisi — with a powerful vision: to provide quality, holistic Catholic education to girls across Trans-Nzoia and prepare them for higher education and life.

Foundation
1980s

Growing Reputation

In its early years, the school built a steady reputation for academic discipline and Christian values. Student numbers grew as word spread across Kenya of the school's commitment to holistic education. The school's close proximity to Suwerwa gave it a unique identity tied to faith, service, and the love of God.

Growth
1980s

First KCSE & Academic Expansion

The first KCSE cohort sat their examinations, establishing the school's academic tradition. Classrooms, dormitories, and the school chapel were expanded. The school began building its strong academic reputation alongside its commitment to spiritual formation.

Expansion
1990s

National School Status

A pivotal decade: St. Francis Girls' Secondary School was elevated to National School status, attracting students from across Kenya. New laboratories, a library, and sports facilities were constructed. The school's reputation for producing well-rounded, disciplined young women attracted students from every corner of the country.

National Status
2000s2010s

International Academic Excellence

SFGS established itself on the world stage, sending students to represent Kenya in prestigious international competitions including the World Scholar's Cup, the International Mathematical Olympiad, and the International Physics Olympiad. Academic and sporting achievements at both national and international levels cemented the school's status as one of Kenya's finest.

International Recognition
Ongoing

A Legacy of Notable Alumni

Over the decades, SFGS has produced a large number of successful graduates who have gone on to excel in medicine, politics, business, education, and many other fields. The school's culture of fostering leadership, innovation, and entrepreneurship has created an alumni network that is highly respected across Kenya and beyond providing mentorship and networking opportunities for current students.

Alumni Legacy

SFGS Today

St. Francis Girls' Secondary School, Suwerwa today stands as a thriving institution with over 1,200 students, offering a wide range of academic programmes and extracurricular activities. The school's core commitment to holistic, Catholic-based education that produces women of faith, excellence, and service — has never wavered.
